# LLM Interview Scoring Rubric

Use this rubric to score answers in mock interviews.

## Score bands

- `9-10`: strong hire signal; precise, structured, includes tradeoffs and metrics
- `7-8`: passable to strong; mostly correct, some missing depth
- `5-6`: partially correct; shallow, generic, or weak on tradeoffs
- `3-4`: major gaps; confused terminology or no practical detail
- `1-2`: incorrect or non-answer

## What to look for

### Technical correctness

- are concepts defined correctly?
- are important distinctions preserved?
- are claims internally consistent?

### Depth

- does the answer move beyond textbook definitions?
- does it mention tradeoffs, failure modes, and edge cases?

### Practicality

- does the user mention metrics, scale, systems limits, or real constraints?
- is there evidence of actually shipping or debugging something?

### Communication

- is the answer structured?
- is it too long, too vague, or too defensive?

## Feedback template

Use this structure:

1. `Score`
2. `What worked`
3. `What was missing`
4. `How an interviewer would press you`
5. `Stronger answer`

## Red flags

- uses buzzwords without mechanisms
- cannot compare alternatives
- gives no metrics or scale
- cannot explain failures
- overclaims ownership
- confuses training, fine-tuning, inference, and evaluation layers
